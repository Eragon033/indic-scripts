Karnataka (ಕರ್ನಾಟಕ)


Karnataka is the largest state in South India and has a population of over 7 crores. Its capital, Bengaluru, is known as the Silicon Valley of India because it is a major hub for IT companies. Karnataka has a diverse culture revolving around dance, music folk and drama.

Kannada is the official language of Karnataka and is one of the six classical languages of India.



Cuisine

Bisi bele bath is a classic dish of Karnataka. It contains rice, lentils, assorted vegetables, and spices. Kannada is also known for the variety of dosas it serves, including Mysore masala dosa, neer dosa and Devangere benne dosa.

Dance

Karnataka has a variety of dance forms. The traditional dance form of Karnataka is Dollu Kunitha performed during major festivals. It is a lively dance involving quick movements accompanied with singing and drumbeats. Other dance forms include Bharatnatyam, Kuchipudi and Yakshagana.

Festivals

The Hampi Festival is celebrated in November to relive the past traditions and culture of the Vijaynagar Empire. Tourists are drawn to the intricately carved ruins of Hampi and enjoy the dance performances and folk songs showcased by the locals.

Kambala is a traditional buffalo race held annually in most districts of Dakshin Kannada and Udupi of Karnataka. This festival is unique to Karnataka and drives participation from the farming community. Women have now been allowed to participate in Kambala, a traditionally male-dominated sport.
